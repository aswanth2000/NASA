What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to long-term changes in the Earth’s climate, primarily caused by human activities, especially the burning of fossil fuels, which releases carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases into the atmosphere.

Global Temperature Rise

Fact: The Earth's average surface temperature has risen by about 1.2°C since the late 19th century.

Data from the US GHG Center shows a rapid increase in temperatures over the last century, contributing to the melting of ice sheets, rising sea levels, and more extreme weather patterns.

Melting Ice Sheets

Fact: The Greenland and Antarctic ice sheets are losing mass at an accelerating rate.

The rapid melting of polar ice sheets is a direct consequence of global warming, raising sea levels and threatening coastal communities worldwide.

What Can You Do?

The fight against climate change begins with awareness and action. Here's what you can do:

  • Reduce your carbon footprint by driving less and using public transport.
  • Switch to renewable energy sources such as solar and wind power.
  • Support policies and leaders focused on sustainability.

Join the movement and take a stand against climate change. It's time for action.
